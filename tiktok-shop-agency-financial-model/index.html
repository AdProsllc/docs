Key takeaways

  1. 1
    Target is achievable with 20 clients at ~$20k average retainer — implies average client GMV around $1M/month. Full capacity at current pricing delivers $400k/mo exactly.
  2. 2
    $250k GMV tier is the economic sweet spot. Effective take rate peaks at 4.0% here — higher than any tier above. For every ten $250k clients added, we generate $100k at a best-in-class margin of 84%.
  3. 3
    Margin structurally improves with scale. At base retainer ($6,500) margin is 75%; at cap ($30k) it's 94.7%. Every upgrade in tier expands gross profit by more than the retainer delta suggests.
  4. 4
    Take rate compresses above $1M GMV. We drop from 4% at $250k to 1% at $3M — at this tier we're competing with in-house teams and margin-rich Pattern-style commission deals. Pricing cap at $30k leaves material upside on the table above $2M GMV.
  5. 5
    Cost structure is lean but incomplete. $1,600/brand covers only three named roles (Strategist, Affiliate Manager, Shop Coordinator). Real delivery cost likely 2-3x higher once tools, platform fees, pod leads, and back-office allocation are included.

Sales velocity required

To reach full capacity in 10 months, we need ~2 new clients per month on average. This is ambitious for a service at this ACV ($120k-$360k annual value per client). Sales team of 1 AE + founder outbound should be sufficient, but acquisition cost ($10-20k per client estimated) is not modeled here — see Unit Economics tab.

Warning · churn is the hidden killer

At 10% monthly churn (which is not unusual for early-stage B2B agencies without strong retention mechanics), replacing lost clients eats all new sales capacity. Revenue plateaus well below target. Investment in account health, QBRs, and upgrade pathways is arguably the single highest-leverage initiative outside of sales. Benchmark target: <3% monthly logo churn.

Strategic recommendation

The current schedule is well-calibrated for the mid-market ($250k–$1M GMV) but leaves significant revenue on the table at the enterprise end. Raising the cap from $30k to $40k (or tying a thin variable back in above $2M) captures 10-15% more revenue from larger clients without touching the mid-market pricing that's already competitive.

The spreadsheet's $800 / $400 / $400 per-brand allocation represents fractional labor, not full FTEs. One Lead Strategist ($5-8k/mo fully loaded) should cover ~5-6 brands at their fee rate. Gap to flag

The spreadsheet excludes Pod Lead / Director-level headcount, QA/Ops, Sales (AE), and Exec overhead. Fully loaded, these roles likely add $40-60k/month in G&A at 20-client capacity — reducing true operating margin from 92% gross to roughly 75-78% operating margin. This is the gap to watch when comparing reported gross margin to actual cash burn.

Model sensitivity

These unit economics are extremely sensitive to the lifetime assumption. If actual client lifetime is 12 months (8% monthly churn) instead of 24, LTV halves and some tiers cross into borderline territory. Measuring actual cohort retention is the single most important instrumentation investment for this business.

G&A assumption

The $50k/mo G&A baseline includes estimated costs for: 1 founder/CEO (allocated), 1 AE for sales, 1 ops/finance lead, software/tooling (TikTok Shop platform fees, creator management tools, CRM), and office/infrastructure. Below-market at $50k-$100k GMV

Base retainer floor means 6.5-13% take rate at this segment — above the 8-12% norm. Acceptable only if we're clearly premium positioned; otherwise we lose price-sensitive small brands.

At-market at $250k-$1M GMV

Our 2-4% take rate here is right in the competitive fairway for full-stack TikTok Shop agencies. This is our strongest competitive positioning segment.

Below-market above $2M GMV

Dropping to 1-1.5% puts us well below Pattern-style commission models (15-20%). Major upside if we can reprice enterprise with performance fees; significant displacement risk if we don't.

Strategic positioning

We're priced like a mid-market managed service — a sweet spot for the $500k-$2M GMV segment where Pattern is too expensive and Stack Influence is too thin. Lean into this positioning with mid-market brand case studies. Reconsider the $30k cap for enterprise brands to capture fair share of their economics.
