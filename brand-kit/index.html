02 — Logo

Logo & Wordmark

The Ad Pros wordmark is the lowercase "ad pros" logotype with a bold yellow rule beneath it — a symbol of the line between strategy and execution. Brand colors: Navy #040735 and Yellow #F9D900.

Primary — Dark BG
Primary — Light BG
Reversed — Yellow BG

Logo Rules

✓ Do
Use approved logo files. Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "a" on all sides. Use dark on light, and vice versa. Minimum 24px digital / 10mm print. Use only Navy, White, or Yellow variants.
× Don't
Recreate or retype the logo in any font. Stretch, skew, or rotate it. Add drop shadows, outlines, or effects. Use below the minimum size. Place on busy or clashing backgrounds.

05 — Spacing System

Spacing System

Built on a 4px base unit. Every layout gap, padding, and margin uses a value from this scale. Keeps visual harmony across screens and materials.

Micro

4–16px. Inline elements, icon padding, tight lists.

Small

20–32px. Component padding, cards, form fields.

Medium

40–64px. Sub-components, feature blocks, modals.

Large

80–192px. Section separators, hero padding, gutters.

09 — Usage Do's & Don'ts

Usage Do's & Don'ts

Quick reference for common brand application questions. When in doubt, reach us at book@adpros.com.

Colors

✓ Do
Use Navy #040735 and Yellow #F9D900 as the hero combination for primary CTAs and headers.
× Don't
Use yellow as a text color on white backgrounds — fails contrast.
✓ Do
Use Blue #1A6FD4 for interactive elements and links.
× Don't
Introduce new brand colors outside the approved palette.
✓ Do
Use the full grey scale for neutral UI, text hierarchy, and backgrounds.
× Don't
Create new "tints" via opacity — use the official palette steps.

Typography

✓ Do
Use Power Grotesk in all digital, print, and marketing materials.
× Don't
Mix more than two font weights in a single layout block.
✓ Do
Maintain hierarchy: Display > Heading > Paragraph > Caption.
× Don't
Use all-caps for body text or paragraphs.
✓ Do
Use letter-spacing −1px on Display styles for brand-accurate headlines.
× Don't
Use decorative or script fonts in Ad Pros materials.

Imagery & Creative

✓ Do
Use high-contrast, energetic imagery reflecting ambition and growth.
× Don't
Use generic stock imagery (handshakes, fake diversity shots).
✓ Do
Apply navy or yellow overlays at ≤60% opacity to reinforce brand presence.
× Don't
Place the logo over busy imagery without a clear-space treatment.
✓ Do
Feature real data, dashboards, and outcomes wherever appropriate.
× Don't
Use competitor brand colors or visual styles.
