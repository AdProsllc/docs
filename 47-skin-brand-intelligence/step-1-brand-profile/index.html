Brand Overview

What They Are

47 Skin is a UK-based anti-blemish skincare brand built around a single unique hero ingredient: Silver Chitoderm®. The "47" in the name is the atomic number of silver — a deliberate signal that there's science behind every product. Founded in 2018 by Nic Taylor, the brand grew from a personal crisis into the UK's fastest-growing skincare company (Sunday Times 100, 2024 & 2025), with 99.38% annual sales growth over three consecutive years.

The brand is now expanding into the US market, where it has an established Shopify storefront (us.47skin.com) but has seen limited traction — US ad spend sits at ~$7K/month vs. a much larger UK footprint. AdPros is engaging to build and scale the US market from scratch.

Founder Story

Why this matters for creative: The founder story IS the brand's most powerful ad hook. Nic's story — spots so bad he couldn't leave the house, grandmother's cream changing everything — is raw, emotional, and universally relatable. It's not background context. It should be front-and-center in US creative.

Nic Taylor lived with severe breakouts through his teens and early twenties. At his lowest, his spots were so bad he couldn't leave the house. His grandmother sent him a locally-made cream containing silver and chitosan. Within four days, his skin recovered in a way no product or medication had achieved in years. Nic tracked down the scientist behind the formula, partnered with him, and launched 47 Skin in 2018.

Within seven months, they hit £1 million in sales. The day before his grandmother's funeral, a major UK luxury retailer called. A year later, the UK's largest pharmacy chain came knocking after a company director's family member had used the product. The brand now serves 500,000+ customers and has been recognized by the Sunday Times as one of the UK's fastest-growing companies two years running.

Guarantee & Risk Reversal

47-day money-back guarantee — aligned with the brand name and hero ingredient. Strong risk reversal that is currently under-utilised in ads and landing pages. The trial program also allows customers to try at home before being charged — a powerful conversion lever not yet pushed in US creative.

⚠ Gap: Offer clarity is weak on both ads AND landing pages. The 47-day money-back guarantee, the subscription discount, and the tiered incentive ladder are not clearly communicated. Customers landing on the PDP don't see a compelling reason to commit. This is a direct revenue leak.

Brand DNA

More of This

  • Efficacy and proof — clinical results, before/afters
  • Real people, real transformations, no filters
  • Emotional storytelling (founder story, customer stories)
  • Science credibility (Silver Chitoderm® explained simply)
  • Confidence and empowerment post-blemish
  • Avatar-specific messaging (moms, teens, adult breakouts)
  • Direct-response urgency in image creative

Less of This

  • Generic "do you have breakouts?" mass market messaging
  • Overly polished, aspirational beauty aesthetics
  • Restrained video scripts (compliance-fear-driven)
  • Hacky, aggressive DTC tactics (Norris Organics style)
  • Obviously AI-generated imagery
  • UK-only creative assets running in US market
  • Vague offers with no clear value summary

Compliance & Claims Guardrails

47 Skin operates in a highly regulated space for the US market. Claims that work in the UK may not be permissible in the US. AdPros must work within these boundaries — and find ways to be emotionally compelling without making clinical claims.

❌ Avoid
→ Use instead: "Supports clearer-looking skin" / "Visibly reduces the appearance of blemishes"
❌ "Cures acne"
→ "Helps clear stubborn blemishes from the first use"
❌ "100% bacteria killed"
→ "Clinically shown to target blemish-causing bacteria"
❌ Pharmaceutical framing
→ Cosmetic/skincare framing — "appearance of" language
⚠ Azelaic Acid
→ Must include pregnancy/breastfeeding warning in copy. Not for Treatment Serum TOF ads.
