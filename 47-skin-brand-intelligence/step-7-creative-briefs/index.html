H
Visual Direction

Visual Concept

A warm, candid moment between mother and teenage daughter — daughter looking at herself in a bathroom mirror with quiet, newfound confidence. Mother standing slightly behind, hand on shoulder. Real. Unpolished. Emotional.

Imagery Style

Real photography (not renders, not influencer-polished). Slightly imperfect lighting adds authenticity. No studio backdrops. The goal is for Petra to feel like she's watching a real family — not an ad.

Color Guidance

Warm whites and soft cream tones. Gentle natural light from a bathroom window. Avoid cold, clinical tones. No blue-white LED light. Aim for the warm morning-light quality of a real family home.

Composition Notes

Portrait orientation (4:5 or 9:16) — daughter's face in center frame, mother's face partially visible over the shoulder. Both in-frame but daughter is the hero. Mirror reflection can be used for depth. Keep text overlay in safe zone — see platform specs below.

Brand DNA reminder: 47 Skin stands for "no filters, no retouching, real people." Any visual treatment must honor that. Skin texture is allowed to be visible. The transformation is real — the creative should feel real too.

C
Strategic Foundation

Objective

Break through ad fatigue by eliminating the visual cues of advertising. Raw, first-person UGC has zero "brand filter" — Petra's guard comes down immediately. Converts cold traffic by making the ad feel like organic peer content.

Hook Mechanic

First-person direct-to-camera opener OR bold quote card with verbatim VOC language. Opens with the frustration state — "I tried everything" — then delivers resolution fast. No build-up. Petra recognises herself in the opening line and commits to watching.

This creative should look and feel like it was filmed on a phone at home. No studio. No ring light (or very subtle ring light only). No professional-level color grade. The "produced UGC" aesthetic — real but watchable.

Format Options

Option 1: Video — mom talking directly to camera, casual, slightly imperfect, real. 20–30 seconds.

Option 2: Static quote card — bold white or yellow text on dark or natural background with mom's photo. Verbatim VOC quote as headline.

C
Strategic Foundation

Structural Formula

Problem: Everything failed — prescriptions, serums, viral products.
Fix: 47 Skin's Silver Chitoderm® targets the actual source — blemish-causing bacteria — rather than symptoms.
Outcome: Visible results within weeks. Teen's confidence restored. Parent's search ends here.

Why This Works

The P→F→O structure is a proven high-conversion storytelling arc because it validates the viewer's experience first, then provides a credible mechanism of action, then delivers an emotional payoff. All three stages are in Petra's language.

Competitive Differentiation

This brief implicitly positions competitors as treating symptoms, not causes — without naming them. The key insight is: "most products mask blemishes; 47 Skin targets the bacteria that cause them." That distinction is scientifically defensible and emotionally clarifying for a mom who's tried everything.

Tone

This brief has the most direct, almost journalistic voice of the three. It's not soft and emotional — it's specific and proof-driven. The emotional warmth comes from the outcome, not the framing. Think: a confident friend sharing a discovery, not a brand making claims.
