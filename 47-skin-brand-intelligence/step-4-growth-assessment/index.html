2. Category Detail

Category 3: Funnel
Acquisition architecture — TOF/MOF/BOF definition, landing pages, entry points
● Failing

Key Findings

PDP is the only landing destination. No listicle, no quiz, no advertorial, no avatar-specific landing pages tested in the US market. Every dollar of ad spend drives to a product detail page — the hardest possible conversion surface.
UK quiz funnel not replicated for US. "Find Out What Your Skin Is Telling You" exists in the UK market but has never been built for the US. This is a known gap and the highest-leverage funnel infrastructure investment available.
UK pages serving US traffic. Redirect issues mean US visitors land on UK-optimized pages — wrong pricing, wrong messaging, wrong cultural framing. This is leaking conversion rate silently.
No MOF retargeting infrastructure. Page visitors, add-to-carts, and video viewers are not being served dedicated MOF creative. Warm audiences are being abandoned.
Incentive ladder exists but isn't communicated. $15/$65/$130/$200 thresholds are set but not prominent in ads or landing pages — potential AOV lever being wasted.

Recommendations

R1
Quiz Funnel Build (US). Replicate and localize the UK "What's Your Skin Telling You" quiz for the US market. Route to Trial ($25), 2-Step ($81), or 3-Step ($119) based on skin type and severity. Target: live within 30 days.
R2
US-Specific Landing Pages. Build avatar-specific pages: Mom TOF page (teen + adult dual story), Adult Breakout page (frustrated with OTC, tried everything angle), and a Silver Science education page. No UK redirects.
R3
MOF Retargeting Isolation. Separate retargeting audiences (site visitors, video viewers, add-to-carts) into dedicated ad sets with MOF-specific creative. Do not mix cold and warm in the same campaign.
R4
Surface Incentive Ladder in Ads + LP. Feature the free gift at $15, free shipping at $65, and free BHA at $130 prominently in both ad copy and landing page headers. Quick AOV lift.
Category 5: Ad Creative
Creative diversity, testing velocity, UGC mix, format coverage, storytelling presence
● Failing

Key Findings

Image-only at scale; zero video creative running. All competitors are using video as primary or secondary format. 47 Skin's TikTok GMV Max result with silver ingredient proves video converts — but it's not being scaled.
No UGC programme. Hero Cosmetics runs 60% UGC. Curology runs 30%. 47 Skin runs 0%. The brand ethos is "real people, real results, no filters" — but the ads don't reflect that.
Low testing velocity vs. competitors. No documented creative testing cadence. Competitors test 10–30+ concepts per month. Without a systematic testing engine, performance plateaus are inevitable as audiences saturate.
Founder story completely absent from paid ads. Nic Taylor's grandmother's cream origin story is one of the strongest brand narratives in the category. It's not running anywhere in paid media.
47-day MBG not featured. No competitor offers a 47-day guarantee. This risk reversal could meaningfully lift Trial conversion rate but is currently buried in terms and conditions.
Silver Chitoderm on TikTok GMV Max converted. Proof that the ingredient narrative works. This is a validated angle to scale — not an experiment to run.

Recommendations

R1
UGC Programme Launch. Brief 10 existing customers (mom demographic priority) with a simple prompt: "Before and after — what was your skin like, and what is it like now?" No script. Raw 30–60 second videos. Run as cold TOF. Expected to outperform current images within 2 weeks.
R2
47-Day MBG as Headline Hook. Test a single ad variant where the headline is the guarantee: "47 days to clearer skin — or your money back. No questions." Pair with a before/after image. Zero production cost, immediate test.
R3
Silver Science Education Video. 30-second MOF video: "Why does silver clear blemishes when salicylic acid hasn't?" Reference the clinical studies. Antimicrobial without stripping. No resistance buildup. This is a differentiation moat no competitor can copy.
R4
Creative Testing Cadence. Establish a weekly testing rhythm: 4–6 new concepts per week across 3 hook types (problem agitation, ingredient science, social proof). Kill non-performers at 72 hours. Scale winners at day 5.
R5
Founder Story Series (MOF). 3-part retargeting series: (1) Origin story — grandmother's cream, (2) The science — why silver works, (3) Real results — 3 before/after customer stories. Run to warm audiences only.
Category 1: Offer
Hero offer clarity, offer matrix, unit economics, subscription path, AOV ladder
● Warning

Key Findings

LTV:CAC at ~2:1 vs. 3–4:1 category benchmark. AOV $76, LTV $150 implies roughly 2 purchases per customer lifetime. To reach $300K/month target profitably, LTV must grow — primarily through subscription conversion and bundle upsell.
Subscription discount is only 14%. Industry benchmark for subscription incentive is 15–25%. A 14% recurring discount may not provide sufficient motivation to switch from one-time purchase, especially for a premium-priced product.
4 advertised offers with no clear spend hierarchy. Trial $25, Serum $45, 2-Step $81, 3-Step $119 are all being advertised simultaneously. Without a clear hero offer dominating 70–80% of acquisition spend, testing signals are fragmented.
Trial $25 entry point is strong. Low-friction acquisition offer with a clear upsell path. The Trial → Serum → System ladder is architecturally sound — it just needs the email sequences and creative to execute it.
47-day MBG is a unique risk reversal. No competitor offers this. When featured prominently, this should materially improve Trial conversion rate and reduce purchase anxiety for new customers.

Recommendations

R1
Hero Offer Consolidation. Designate Trial $25 as the sole acquisition hero offer for cold TOF. Run 80% of cold spend to this offer. All other products shift to retargeting/warm audiences only. This focuses testing signal and reduces audience fragmentation.
R2
Subscription Incentive Review. Test increasing autoship discount to 20% or adding a value-add (free gift on first subscription order). The goal: raise subscription conversion rate from unknown baseline to 20%+ of Trial buyers.
R3
Upsell Sequence Build. Build a 7-email post-Trial-purchase sequence with a hard upsell to the 3-Step System at day 10 (when Trial is running low). Price anchor: $119 system vs. $25/month individually. Day 7 urgency offer.
Category 6: Ad Optimization
Scaling rules, kill criteria, retargeting isolation, metric hierarchy, account structure
● Warning

Key Findings

No documented scaling or kill criteria. Decisions on what to scale and what to cut are likely instinct-driven rather than rule-based. Without clear thresholds (e.g., kill at 2x CAC at 72h, scale at 0.8x CAC at 96h), performance degrades as budget increases.
Retargeting likely not isolated from prospecting. If warm audiences are mixed into cold prospecting campaigns, it artificially inflates reported ROAS and masks true cold acquisition performance.
TikTok GMV Max running. Auto-optimized GMV Max is a reasonable initial approach but lacks manual control for creative testing. As spend scales, a hybrid approach (GMV Max + manual creative testing campaigns) will be needed.
NC ROAS as primary KPI is correct. New Customer ROAS focus prevents over-crediting retargeting and subscription revenue. This is the right metric hierarchy for a scaling DTC brand.

Recommendations

R1
Scaling Engine Documentation. Define explicit rules: kill threshold (CAC x2 at 72h), scale threshold (CAC 0.8x at day 5), budget increment (20% per 48h when scaling), and pause trigger (3-day declining CVR). Document and make non-negotiable.
R2
Retargeting Isolation. Separate prospecting (cold) and retargeting (warm) into distinct campaigns with dedicated budgets. Cold: 70–75% of budget. Retargeting: 25–30%. Measure separately. Never mix.
R3
Account Structure Audit. Review current Meta account structure to identify audience overlap, redundant ad sets, and budget fragmentation. Clean structure = cleaner signals = better algorithmic optimization.

Category 7: Tracking for Decision Making
Attribution stack, tool configuration, benchmarking, reporting cadence, data gaps
● Warning

Key Findings

Triple Whale shows 10x multiplier vs. Meta-reported ROAS on some campaigns. This is the most critical data signal in the assessment. Someone is lying — and it's likely Meta's self-reported ROAS over-crediting results. Budget decisions cannot be trusted until this is resolved.
Triple Whale in onboarding — not yet configured. Access being shared. Until Triple Whale is live and validated, all performance data must be treated as directional, not definitive.
NC ROAS as primary KPI is the right model. New Customer ROAS focus prevents over-crediting retention and repeat purchases. Core KPIs defined: NC ROAS, CAC, NC Orders, NC AOV, CPM/CPC.
Email platform unknown — email revenue unattributed. If email is driving meaningful post-purchase revenue, it's currently invisible in the attribution model. This inflates paid media's apparent contribution.

Recommendations

R1
Triple Whale Configuration — Priority 0. Complete Triple Whale onboarding before any significant budget scaling decisions. The 10x ROAS discrepancy must be resolved. Until it is, there is a real risk of scaling unprofitable campaigns.
R2
Attribution Reconciliation. Run a 14-day audit comparing Meta-reported ROAS vs. Triple Whale NC ROAS vs. Shopify revenue by channel. Identify the source of the 10x discrepancy. Set the "truth" metric that governs all future budget decisions.
R3
Weekly Reporting Cadence. Establish a weekly metrics review: NC ROAS by campaign, CAC trend, NC orders week-over-week, CPM trend. Monthly: full P&L attribution including email. Quarterly: benchmarking against category standards.
