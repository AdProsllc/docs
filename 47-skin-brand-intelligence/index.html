Brand Snapshot

A founder story the US hasn't heard yet

The origin

Nic Taylor lived with severe breakouts through his teens and early twenties. At his lowest, his skin was so bad he couldn't leave the house. His grandmother sent him a locally-made cream containing silver and chitosan. Within four days, his skin recovered in a way no product or medication had achieved in years.

Nic tracked down the scientist behind the formula, partnered with him, and launched 47 Skin in 2018. Within seven months, they hit £1 million in sales. The day before his grandmother's funeral, a major UK luxury retailer called. A year later, the UK's largest pharmacy chain came knocking. The brand now serves 500,000+ customers globally and was named to the Sunday Times 100 (UK's fastest-growing companies) in both 2024 and 2025.

Target Avatars

Two audiences. One bottle. 60 / 40 creative split.

Petra is the primary avatar — the Mom of a Teen with Breakouts — who carries the highest conversion signal and the emotional white space no competitor is occupying. Sophie is the Adult Breakout Woman — the category's fastest-growing segment.

Primary · 60–70% allocation

Petra — Mom of Teen with Breakouts

Age 38–52. Watching her teenager hide from the camera, cancel plans, wear a hoodie in summer. Has tried the dermatologist, the drugstore, the viral TikTok product. Nothing worked. She is in research mode with her credit card out.

Primary Motivation
  • Protect her child from the confidence damage of breakouts
Biggest Fear
  • Her teen's breakouts leave permanent scars — physical AND emotional
What she responds to
  • Mom-to-mom testimonials and transformation stories
  • Founder science + grandmother-recipe heritage
  • Authority stacking: Vogue, 500,000 customers, Sephora
  • Risk-free trial / money-back guarantee
"I tried everything for my daughter's skin. This is the only thing that worked."
Secondary · 20–30% allocation

Sophie — Adult Breakout Woman

Age 25–40. Adult breakouts affecting her professional confidence. Highly ingredient-aware. Frustrated by products built for oily teen skin that strip her adult barrier. She will research before buying.

Primary Motivation
  • Clear her breakouts without harsh medications that dry out and damage her skin
Biggest Fear
  • Breakouts will always cycle back — she'll never fully be free of them
What she responds to
  • Clinical evidence and ingredient science
  • "Adult skin is different" validation
  • Diversity and hormonal-cycle representation
  • Sensitive-skin + breakout-prone dual benefit
"My skin is sensitive AND breakout-prone. This is the only thing that didn't make it worse."

Creative White Space

Three unoccupied positions 47 Skin can own

01 · The Mom-to-Mom Angle

Zero direct competitors target the Mom of a Teen avatar with dedicated creative. Hero targets Gen Z. Proactiv runs broad celebrity. Curology is one-to-one clinical. The mom + teen dual-narrative is unoccupied and directly mapped to 47 Skin's real customer buying pattern.

02 · Adult Hormonal Breakouts

Category's fastest-growing segment at 14.1% CAGR. 50% of women in their 20s and 35% in their 30s deal with hormonal breakouts. Most brands still speak to teens. Silver Chitoderm's kills-bacteria-AND-hydrates profile is exactly what this audience needs.

03 · Silver Science as Education

Nobody else has Silver Chitoderm®. A 30–45 second MOF education ad — "Why does silver work when nothing else has?" — converts the skeptical, research-mode audience that has tried and failed with standard blemish products.
