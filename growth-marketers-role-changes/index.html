Role Changes — May 2026

Internal

The Growth Marketer role
is changing.

Here's how our updated services and agency model change your role.

What's Different
You're running client meetings
Ownership
Growth Strategists have shared the client-facing load on meetings. Going forward, you run your client calls — setting the agenda, presenting performance, and owning the follow-through.
The Growth Strategist role is being wound down
Structure
As the team transitions, strategic ownership shifts more directly to Growth Marketers. For clients you currently share with a Growth Strategist, that collaboration continues as normal. For new clients going forward, you're the primary owner from day one.
Client capacity is increasing
Capacity
Capacity is measured in platforms, not just clients — and the target is 20 Tier 1 platforms or 10 Tier 2 platforms per Growth Marketer. The AI systems being built are specifically designed to make this realistic.
A CRO department is being built
Coming Soon
We're actively hiring for a dedicated CRO role. When it's live, you'll brief CRO the same way you brief the Creative Team — with data and a specific ask. It expands what you can deliver for clients without adding to your execution load.
Client meetings are moving to bi-weekly
Cadence
Weekly client calls are being phased out in favour of bi-weekly. Less time in recurring meetings means more time for analysis, testing, and actual execution.
AI is being integrated across your workflow
Systems
Reporting, analysis, meeting prep, presentations, performance alerts — these are being automated. The goal is to return enough time that higher client capacity becomes operational, not just theoretical.

What you own.

With more strategic ownership comes clearer accountability. Here's how the role is defined.

Core Responsibilities
Client Relationship
You are the primary point of contact for your clients. The client experience — communication, expectations, escalation — is yours to manage.
Testing Methodology
You run the testing engine. What gets tested, in what order, with what hypothesis and budget allocation.
Scaling Decisions
You decide when to scale and when to pull back. Spend decisions within your portfolio are yours to make.
Strategic Prioritization
You determine what matters most for each client at any given time — what to push on, what to hold, and what to escalate.
How You Work with the Creative Team
Creative strategy is the Creative Team's domain. Your role is to be the performance layer that informs their decisions.
→Surface what's working, what's failing, and what needs a refresh — with data behind it
→The Creative Team responds with concept direction, brief guidance, and creative strategy
→You need to understand creative strategy well enough to contextualize results for clients, ask the right questions, and flag when something isn't landing
Capacity Model
Capacity is measured in platforms, not clients. Tiers are based on monthly ad spend per platform.

Goal 2
