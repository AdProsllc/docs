The Hybrid Model

TurboTax Live combines TurboTax's DIY software with on-demand access to credentialed human tax experts (CPAs and Enrolled Agents), delivered virtually. The product exists in two tiers targeting fundamentally different customer needs.

Expert Assist (rebranded from "Live Assisted" for the 2026 tax season) lets customers prepare their own returns using TurboTax software while accessing unlimited on-demand help from tax experts via phone, chat, or one-way video. Every Expert Assist return receives a final comprehensive review by a human expert before filing. This tier targets the "anxious DIY filer."

Expert Full Service (rebranded from "Live Full Service") is a complete handoff: a matched tax expert prepares, reviews, signs, and files the entire return. The customer uploads documents, meets virtually (or in-person at one of approximately 600 expert offices and 20 branded TurboTax storefronts), reviews the completed return, and approves filing. This tier directly competes with H&R Block, Jackson Hewitt, and local CPA firms — the $35 billion assisted tax preparation market encompassing roughly 85 million professionally prepared returns annually.

Key insight: TurboTax Live sits inside Intuit's Consumer Group segment, which generated $4.87B in FY2025. TT Live's $2.0B represents 41% of Consumer Group revenue — up from ~30% a year earlier — and Intuit's CFO has stated it will become the majority of Consumer Group revenue in the coming years.

Revenue Math: 39M Returns & Rising ARPR

Total TurboTax returns filed were approximately 39.2 million in FY2025 — actually declining 2% YoY. This is a deliberate choice: Intuit has pursued shedding low-value customers to increase average revenue per return. "Pay-nothing" customers dropped from over 10M in FY2023 to roughly 8M in FY2025. Meanwhile, online paying units grew 6% and ARPR increased approximately 13%.

The ARPR differential between DIY and Live is stark. A blended DIY customer generates roughly $50–90. A TurboTax Live customer generates an estimated $200–250 — approximately 3–4x the DIY average.

Unit Economics: W-2 Experts & AI Cost Compression

The cost structure of TurboTax Live differs fundamentally from DIY TurboTax, which is essentially pure software with near-zero marginal cost and estimated gross margins of 90–95%. TurboTax Live introduces human labor as a significant cost component.

Intuit employs approximately 15,000 tax experts as W-2 employees, not 1099 contractors. Average compensation runs approximately $40/hour ($83K annualized), with CPA-credentialed Full Service experts earning around $54/hour ($112K annualized). Fully loaded with benefits and overhead (30–40% burden rate), the effective cost per expert hour runs $52–70.

The price premium for Expert Assist over DIY ranges from $60–79 at equivalent complexity levels. Expert Full Service uses forms-based pricing that scales with return complexity — aligning cost-to-serve with revenue per return.

Intuit's upsell mechanics begin before a customer selects a product. The landing experience uses a quiz-based recommendation system that typically defaults to recommending Expert Assist. Complexity triggers (investment income, self-employment, itemized deductions) force mandatory upgrades to paid tiers. The "start free, pay when you file" model creates significant sunk-cost friction.

Additional revenue extraction includes a $40 refund processing fee, audit defense add-ons, MAX protection packages, and Refund Advance loans that drive Credit Karma Money account openings. The "Beat Your Price" guarantee (10% savings vs. previous tax preparer) directly targets H&R Block and CPA client conversion.

Growth Arc: $0 → $1B → $2B

TurboTax Live traces its origins to SmartLook (January 2016). The product officially launched in November 2017. When Sasan Goodarzi became CEO in January 2019, he made TurboTax Live central to his vision of transforming Intuit into an "AI-driven expert platform."

The Full Service tier launched in December 2020, marking the critical expansion from "help me do my taxes" to "do my taxes for me." Full Service customers more than tripled in FY2022, the same year TurboTax Live crossed the $1 billion revenue threshold.

Three levers drove the jump from ~$1.3B to $2.0B in a single year:

  • Customer volume surged 24% — fueled by Credit Karma integration (3x higher TurboTax starts, zero-click login rates from 5% to 70%), 600 expert offices, and the Beat Your Price offer
  • ARPR expanded ~13% — through mix shift toward higher-priced Full Service and faster-refund-access features
  • AI-driven productivity gains — serving 24% more customers without proportionally scaling the 15,000-expert workforce
Early FY2026 signal: Q1 FY2026 (ending October 2025) showed TurboTax Live revenue growing 51% year-over-year, suggesting the acceleration is continuing.

AI enables an "AI-first, human-escalation" model where Intuit Assist handles routine questions and pre-call preparation, while human experts focus on complex judgment calls. Each expert can serve more customers per hour — the key to scaling a 15,000-person workforce to serve millions of Live customers.

Intuit's roadmap points toward agentic AI — autonomous AI agents for customer service, payment processing, and tax preparation that communicate with each other and escalate to humans only when necessary. The long-term trajectory appears aimed at approaching DIY-like margins on assisted products by minimizing — though never eliminating — the human touch.

Path to $3B+ : The Key Levers

The $2.0 billion FY2025 milestone is standalone TurboTax Live revenue, not a blended segment figure. It was reached through a three-part formula: aggressive customer acquisition (24% growth), rising ARPR (13%), and AI-compressed cost-to-serve (20% expert time reduction).

The path forward hinges on three structural advantages:

  • $35 billion TAM — TurboTax Live has barely penetrated the ~85M returns currently done by traditional preparers. Intuit claims just 6% penetration of total addressable market.
  • AI cost curve — every improvement in Intuit Assist widens the margin spread between what customers pay and what it costs to serve them, creating a self-reinforcing investment cycle.
  • Platform flywheel — Credit Karma (lead gen) → TurboTax (monetization) → QuickBooks (business customer expansion) creates cross-platform compounding.
Bottom line: With Q1 FY2026 showing 51% YoY growth and physical expansion to 600+ expert offices, TurboTax Live is plausibly on track to reach $2.5–3.0 billion in FY2026. The business that launched as an experiment in virtual tax assistance eight years ago has become the single most important growth engine in a $19 billion company.
