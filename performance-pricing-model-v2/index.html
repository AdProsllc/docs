How the model works

Three components stack to align fees with results.
01 — FOUNDATION

Base + Variable Fee

$10,000 flat retainer plus a tiered media buying fee (4% on $62.5K–$500K, 3% on $500K–$1M, scaling down at higher volume).

Base retainer$10,000 Variable @ $300K$9,500 Pre-multiplier total$19,500
02 — MULTIPLIER

CPA Performance

Both fees scale by a multiplier driven by actual CPA. Calibrated so $60 CPA = standard flat-fee pricing. Capped at 1.50× (great), floored at 0.45× (poor).

$30 CPA1.50× $50 CPA1.00× $60 CPA (target)0.71× $80 CPA0.45×
03 — UNLOCK

Spend Bonus

An additional % of monthly spend that only unlocks when CPA hits $50 or better. Rewards scaling spend at high efficiency.

$50 CPA0.25% of spend $40 CPA0.50% of spend $30 CPA0.75% of spend
!

Why this version is fair to both sides. At $60 CPA the client pays exactly what a flat-fee retainer would cost — there's no premium for "trying" the performance model. The asymmetry is intentional: the upside ($20K at $50 CPA) is bigger than the downside ($10K at $75 CPA) because hitting $50 CPA at $300K spend creates real, measurable value the client wouldn't otherwise have. The agency also has a real floor — at $75 CPA we're still earning $10K, enough to cover team time, but a clear signal that something needs to change.
