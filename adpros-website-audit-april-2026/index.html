Section 01

Technical Audit

The site is built on Webflow with solid fundamentals. Load times are acceptable, but there are SEO and accessibility gaps that hurt rankings and user experience.

High

23 Images Missing Alt Text

Nearly half the images on the homepage have no alt attributes. This hurts SEO, screen reader accessibility, and image search visibility.

Fix: Add descriptive alt text to every image. For logos: "Ka'Chava logo". For founder photos: "Nehal Kazim, CEO of Ad Pros".
High

No Structured Data (Schema Markup)

Zero JSON-LD schema on any page. No Organization schema, no LocalBusiness, no Article schema for blog posts. Google is flying blind on what this site represents.

Fix: Add Organization schema (name, logo, URL, sameAs links), LocalBusiness for the Wyoming address, and Article schema for every blog post.
Medium

Blog Page Loads in 6.6 Seconds

The blog loads 6.6s vs the homepage at 1.4s. 27 scripts are loaded. This is a massive bounce-rate risk.

Fix: Defer non-critical scripts, lazy-load images below the fold, audit third-party embeds.
Medium

No XML Sitemap or robots.txt Verification

Could not verify sitemap.xml or confirm robots.txt configuration. Search engines may not be indexing all pages efficiently.

Fix: Ensure /sitemap.xml exists and is submitted to Google Search Console. Verify robots.txt allows all important paths.
Low

28 Scripts on Homepage

Excessive scripts increase render-blocking time. Many are likely Webflow boilerplate and analytics.

Fix: Audit and remove unused scripts. Move analytics to Google Tag Manager with async loading.

Section 02

Copy & Messaging Audit

The copy is confident and brand-aligned, but it lacks specificity, emotional hooks, and conversion psychology. It reads like an agency that knows what it's doing but forgets to tell visitors exactly what to do next.

Critical

Only One CTA Across the Entire Site: "Join Waitlist"

Every single page funnels to the same waitlist. There is no "Book a call," "Apply now," "See pricing," or "Talk to our team." This dramatically limits conversion paths for visitors at different intent stages.

Fix: Add a secondary "Book a Strategy Call" CTA on every page. Create a dedicated /apply or /book page with a Calendly or custom scheduling form. The waitlist is fine for cold traffic, but warm traffic should be able to talk to someone immediately.
Critical

Typo in Featured Case Study: "$2,0000,000+"

The SupportPets case study shows "$2,0000,000+" — an extra zero. On a site selling precision and performance, this erodes trust instantly.

Fix: Correct to "$2,000,000+". Better yet, add specific timeframes ("$2M/month in ad spend as of Q1 2025") for credibility.
High

No Pricing, Packages, or Minimums Anywhere

Potential clients have no idea if Ad Pros works with $50K/month brands or $5M/month brands. This creates friction and likely filters out qualified leads who assume it's "too expensive" or "not for us yet."

Fix: Add a "Who We Work With" or "Investment" section with minimums (e.g., "We partner with brands spending $100K+/month on ads"). Even ballpark ranges build trust.
High

Case Studies Are Just Numbers, Not Stories

"$4M+ per month in profitable ad spend" is impressive, but there's no before/after, no timeline, no challenge/solution narrative, no client quote. It's a stat, not a story.

Fix: Build dedicated case study pages: /case-studies/kachava with a narrative arc (Challenge → Approach → Results → Client Quote → Metrics). Add video testimonials if possible.
Medium

"Unreasonable Growth" Is Undefined

The homepage says "unreasonable growth means growing 5-20% of top line monthly." That's buried in copy. It should be a headline or visual stat card.

Fix: Turn this into a visual: "We grow brands 5-20% monthly. Here's how." with a mini chart or timeline.
Medium

Founder Section Is Buried at the Bottom

Nehal's bio is the 8th section on the homepage. For a founder-led agency, this should be much higher — ideally in the first 3 sections.

Fix: Move the founder section above the fold or immediately after case studies. Add a photo of Nehal (not just the name). People buy from people.
Low

"Certified Business Partners" Section Appears Empty

The homepage has a "Certified Business Partners" label but no visible partner logos in the captured view. If this is an empty state, it looks broken.

Fix: Add Meta, Google, TikTok, Taboola partner badges. Or remove the section until partner assets are ready.
Section 03

UI/UX Audit

The dark-mode aesthetic is premium and consistent. But there are UX friction points that confuse users and reduce engagement.

High

Services Tabs Hide Critical Content

The Media Buying / Creatives / Data / Strategy tabs on both the homepage and services page hide 75% of the value proposition behind clicks. Most users will never see Creatives, Data, or Strategy content.

Fix: Unroll the tabs into a vertical scrollable section or accordion. Each service deserves its own visual block with an icon, headline, and 2-sentence description — all visible without interaction.
High

Waitlist Form Is a Black Box

The waitlist asks only "Are you currently advertising? YES/NO" as Step 1. There's no preview of what comes next, no value proposition for completing it, and no progress indicator. Users don't know if they're signing up for a newsletter or applying for a $50K engagement.

Fix: Add a progress bar ("Step 1 of 4"). Preview the questions. Add micro-copy: "Tell us about your brand and we'll reach out within 48 hours if it's a fit." Consider a lighter first step: email + monthly ad spend range.
Medium

Contact Form Button Is Disabled by Default

The "Send Message" button on the contact page starts disabled. Users may think the form is broken if they don't realize validation is required. This is a friction point.

Fix: Enable the button by default and show validation errors on submit. Or add clear inline validation with green checkmarks.
Medium

Footer Newsletter Button Is Also Disabled

Same pattern: the "Get started" newsletter button is disabled until an email is entered. This is a minor friction but unnecessary.

Fix: Enable the button. Validate on submit. Show a success message inline.
Medium

Platform Icons Are Text-Only, Not Visual

The "Your Audience Awaits" section lists 11 platforms as plain text. No icons, no platform colors, no visual recognition. This is a huge missed opportunity for visual trust.

Fix: Add official platform icons (Facebook, TikTok, Google, etc.) in a horizontal scrollable row or 3x4 grid. Use each platform's brand color on hover.
Low

Map Section on Contact Page Is Invisible

The "International Network" map is extremely dark and blends into the background. It doesn't communicate the global presence effectively.

Fix: Use a lighter map style (Mapbox dark theme with brighter dots) or replace with a simple world map graphic with highlighted countries and team headshots.
Section 04

Conversion Audit

This is where the site bleeds the most. A $250M+ agency should have a conversion rate infrastructure that matches its ambition. Right now, it doesn't.

Critical

No Lead Magnet or Gated Content

There is nothing to download, no free tool, no calculator, no guide. Every visitor who isn't ready to join a waitlist bounces with zero value exchanged. The blog has great content but no email capture on posts.

Fix: Create a lead magnet: "The $100M Ad Creative Framework" PDF, "Ecommerce Media Buying Calculator," or "The 11-Channel Growth Scorecard." Gate it behind email. Promote it in the blog sidebar and as a bottom-of-post CTA.
Critical

No Chat Widget or Live Response Channel

High-intent visitors have no way to ask a quick question. No Intercom, no Drift, no Calendly chat, nothing. They either join a waitlist into the void or leave.

Fix: Add a chat widget (Intercom, Crisp, or Tidio) with business hours and an away message. Or add a "Questions?" floating button that opens a simple FAQ + contact form.
High

No Exit-Intent or Retention Mechanism

When a visitor moves to close the tab, nothing happens. No popup, no "Wait, download our free guide," no discount offer, no newsletter signup prompt. This is the highest-ROI opportunity on the entire site.

Fix: Implement an exit-intent modal on the blog and homepage: "Before you go — get our free $100M Creative Framework." Convert 5-15% of abandoning visitors.
High

No Social Proof Beyond 3 Stats and Logos

The site has logo marquees and 3 case study numbers. No video testimonials, no client quotes with faces, no "As seen in" press logos, no Trustpilot/G2 ratings, no "Voted #1 Agency" badges. For a high-ticket service, this is insufficient.

Fix: Add a testimonials section with client headshots, names, and titles. Embed 2-3 short video testimonials. Add "As Featured In" press logos if applicable. Consider a G2 or Clutch profile with reviews.
Medium

No Urgency or Scarcity Signals

A waitlist implies exclusivity, but it's not communicated. There's no "Limited to 12 clients per quarter" or "Next intake: June 2026." Without scarcity, there's no reason to act now.

Fix: Add scarcity micro-copy: "We partner with 12 brands per quarter. 3 spots remaining for Q3 2026." Or add a deadline: "Applications close May 15."
Medium

No Retargeting Pixel Confirmation

While Google Analytics is present, there's no visible Meta Pixel or TikTok Pixel. For an agency that runs ads, not retargeting website visitors is leaving money on the table.

Fix: Install Meta Pixel, TikTok Pixel, and Google Ads remarketing tag. Create custom audiences for: all visitors, blog readers, waitlist starters (did not finish), contact page visitors.
Section 05

Strategic Recommendations

Here is the prioritized playbook. These are not just fixes, they're strategic moves that compound.

01 — PHASE 1 (This Week)

Fix the Typos & Enable Forms

Correct the "$2,0000,000" typo. Enable all disabled form buttons by default. Add inline validation. These are 10-minute fixes with immediate trust impact.

02 — PHASE 1 (This Week)

Add a "Book a Call" Path

Create a /book or /strategy-call page with Calendly embedded. Add "Book a Free Strategy Call" as a secondary CTA on every page. This unlocks warm traffic immediately.

03 — PHASE 1 (This Week)

Install Chat + Exit Intent

Add Crisp or Intercom chat. Deploy an exit-intent modal offering the lead magnet (see #06). These two tools alone can 2-3x lead capture.

04 — PHASE 2 (This Month)

Build Real Case Study Pages

Move beyond stats. Create /case-studies/kachava with a full narrative: the challenge, the Ad Pros approach, creative samples, results with timelines, and a client quote. Link these from the homepage.

05 — PHASE 2 (This Month)

Unroll the Services Tabs

Replace the hidden tabs with visible service cards. Each card gets an icon, headline, 2-line description, and a "Learn more" link. Show all 4 services without clicks.

06 — PHASE 2 (This Month)

Create a High-Value Lead Magnet

"The Ecommerce Ad Creative Scorecard" or "11-Channel Media Buying Checklist." Gate it behind email. Promote it in the blog sidebar, footer, and exit-intent modal. Build the email list.

07 — PHASE 3 (Next Quarter)

Add Video Testimonials & Founder Content

Move Nehal's section up. Add a 60-second founder video to the homepage. Collect 3 video testimonials from Ka'Chava, SupportPets, and Alpha Lion. Video converts 3-5x better than text.

08 — PHASE 3 (Next Quarter)

Build a "Who We Work With" Filter

Add a section or page that clearly states: minimum ad spend, ideal brand stage, industries served, and what's included. This pre-qualifies leads and reduces unfit inquiries.

09 — PHASE 3 (Next Quarter)

SEO Content Expansion

The blog has 5 posts. Scale to 2-3 posts per month targeting high-intent keywords: "ecommerce facebook ad agency," "tiktok shop agency," "creative production for dtc brands." Add schema markup to every post.

10 — PHASE 3 (Next Quarter)

Platform Visual Overhaul

Replace text-only platform lists with official icons in a colorful grid. Add platform-specific mini-pages (/platforms/tiktok, /platforms/meta) with tailored messaging. This captures platform-specific search traffic.

The Bottom Line

Ad Pros has a visually strong website that communicates credibility. But it operates like a static brochure when it should be a conversion engine. The gap between the brand's $250M+ track record and its current web experience is significant.

The good news: every major issue has a clear fix, and many are quick wins. The site doesn't need a redesign. It needs strategic layering: more CTAs, better social proof, faster forms, and active conversion mechanisms.

"The best agencies don't just run great ads. They have great funnels. Your website is your first ad. Make it convert."

Next step: Pick 3 quick wins from the checklist above and ship them this week. Measure the delta in waitlist signups and contact form submissions. Then layer in the Phase 2 and Phase 3 recommendations.
